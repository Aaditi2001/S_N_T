01 Reheating Furnace Area

The Reheating Furnace Area refers to the section in a steel production or rolling mill plant where metal billets, blooms, or slabs are heated to a high temperature (typically around 1100–1300°C) before being passed through the rolling mill for shaping.

02 Gear Box

A Gear Box is a mechanical transmission device used to transfer power and torque from a motor to machinery, such as rolling mills, conveyors, or other industrial equipment in a steel production plant.

Mill Stands are essential components in a rolling mill where hot or cold metal (usually billets, slabs, or bars) is compressed and shaped between rotating rolls.

Shears are cutting machines used in steel production and rolling mills to cut, crop, or divide metal products (such as billets, bars, rods, or sheets) into desired lengths or sections during or after the rolling process.
